# Human-Crowd-Stampede-Early-Warning-System

every time i see news about a stampede in india i always wonder why nobody saw it coming. it never just happens suddenly, the crowd was building up for minutes before it turned deadly and everyone could see it getting denser but nobody had a number, nobody had a threshold, nobody had a system that said stop. so i built one.

the idea is a 10x10 grid of pressure sensors on the floor of a venue like a temple courtyard or a stadium entry. each sensor reads how much weight is on that one square meter of floor and the system converts that into crowd density. when density crosses 6 people per sqm in a zone the system calculates a danger score, predicts how many seconds before it hits critical compression, and tells security exactly which direction to evacuate people toward. theres a live heatmap on the dashboard that shows every cell colour coded from green to dark red and a blinking alert banner that escalates through four levels, safe, warning, danger and critical. you can also simulate real scenarios by clicking buttons, close an exit gate and watch the north side go red, trigger a panic wave from a corner and watch it spread across the grid, create a bottleneck and see the middle columns compress.

i always thought instrumentation was only about industrial processes but this showed me that the same sensor and signal processing logic can be applied to human safety in a completely different way. the maths behind it is the same, density thresholds, rate of change, diffusion modelling. just the context is different and the stakes are much higher.
